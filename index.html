Welcome to the Sylver Coinage Browser Game.

The rules:
Player 1 names a positive integer, then players take turns naming positive integers that cannot be expressed as a sum of previously named integers. The first player who cannot do so loses.

Invented by John H. Conway, this deceptively simple game is still not completely solved. The computer has been programmed to play semi-optimally, see if you can beat it!

This website and game were built by Liam Chung for MATH180 (Art of Mathematics) taught by Sid Trudeau, at McGill University.

Will you go first, or let the computer go first?

Good luck, and feel free to switch back to the rules at any time! It will preserve your progress.
